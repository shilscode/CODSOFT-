About

Ustad Zakir Hussain (born 9 March 1951) is an Indian tabla player, composer, percussionist, music producer and film actor.
He is the eldest son of tabla player Alla Rakha.
He was awarded the Padma Shri in 1988, the Padma Bhushan in 2002, and the Padma Vibhushan in 2023, by the Government of India.
He was also awarded the Govt of India's Sangeet Natak Akademi Award in 1990, Fellowship of the Sangeet Natak Akademi Fellowship, Ratna Sadsya in 2018. In 1999, he was awarded the United States National Endowment for the Arts' National Heritage Fellowship, the highest award given to traditional artists and musicians.Zakir Hussain Allaraka Qureshi was born on 9 March 1951 in Mumbai (officially known then as Bombay), India. He attended St. Michael's High School in Mahim, and graduated from St. Xavier's College, Mumbai.

Career

1. Collaborations with Renowned Artists: Played on albums of George Harrison, John Handy, Van Morrison, and Earth, Wind & Fire, showcasing his versatility across genres.
2. Planet Drum Album and Grammy Win: Created the acclaimed album "Planet Drum" featuring drummers worldwide, earning the Grammy Award for Best World Music Album in 1992.
3. Global Drum Project and Second Grammy: United with Mickey Hart, Sikiru Adepoju, and Giovanni Hidalgo again for the "Global Drum Project" album, winning another Grammy in 2009.
4. Film Involvement and Awards: Composed, performed, and advised on music for films like "Vanaprastham," earning recognition at prestigious film festivals and awards.
5. Notable Film Soundtracks: Contributed tabla performances to iconic film soundtracks, including "Apocalypse Now" and "Little Buddha."
6. Documentaries and Acting: Starred in documentaries showcasing his musical prowess, such as "Zakir and His Friends" and "The Speaking Hand."
Co-starred in the film "Heat and Dust" and was an associate music director.
7. Founding Member of Tabla Beat Science: Played a pivotal role in the world music supergroup Tabla Beat Science, led by Bill Laswell.
8. Invitation to International Jazz Day at the White House: Invited by President Obama to perform at the International Jazz Day 2016 All-Star Global Concert at the White House.
9. Unique Tabla Maker Relationship: Haridas Vhatkar has crafted Hussain's tablas for over 18 years, a testament to their special musical connection.
10. Philosophy on Music Performances: Expresses the belief that music should be the primary focus of an event, rather than serving as background to other activities.

Book

Nasreen Munni Kabir compiled 15 interview sessions (each lasting about 2 hours) from 2016 to 2017 into the book Zakir Hussain: A Life in Music, which was published in 2018. This book takes the reader through Hussain's life from his youth, his years of intense training, and growth as a musician.

The Legacy

Ustad Alla Rakha Qureshi (29 April 1919 – 3 February 2000), mononymously known as Alla Rakha, was an Indian tabla player who specialised in Hindustani classical music. He was a frequent accompanist of sitar player Pandit Ravi Shankar and was largely responsible for introducing Tabla to the western audience.
